Everything Everywhere all at Once

Overview

Everything Everywhere all at Once is a 2022 American sci-fi with a hint of comedy-drama under the direction of Daniel Kwan and Daniel Scheinert. It follows the life of a Chinese-American immigrant, Evelyn Wang, who is facing difficulties keeping her family together while keeping the business afloat. As if those weren't enough, she also has to deal with the auditing of the IRS. With the absurdity of her situation, she still manages to find herself falling into a more dire situation where she discovers the existence of multiverse and it seems like she is the only one capable of saving it.

Main Characters

Evelyn Quan Wang

Played by Michelle Yeoh. A very troubled mother, wife, business owner, and was suddenly sent into an interdimensional battle. With her journey of travelling these multiverses, she discovers different versions of herself, family, acquaintances, and had extreme reflections of her life.

Waymond Wang

Played by Ke Huy Quan. Evelyn's husband. He has a very bubbly personality and is very kind-hearted, who in each multiverse, take multiple roles from a very caring spouse to a very capable fighter.

Joy Wang

Played by Stephanie Hsu. Evelyn and Waymond's daughter. She always have an argument with her mother which drove her into rejection and negative feelings. In an alternate reality, her variant became the leader of an oppressive group trying to take over everything in the multiverse.

Deirdre Beaubeirde

Played by Jamie Lee Curtis. An IRS inspector pressuring the Wangs to take care of their paperwork and tax problems. Initially implied as a villain, she later on becomes a critical part in the fight and a reason of Evelyn's reflection and realizations.

Plot

The movie follows Evelyn as she is pulled into the multiverse, where she must learn to use the skills of her alternate selves to fight against Jobu Tupaki. Along the way, she confronts her own regrets, reconciles with her family, and learns to accept kindnesss and connection to others.

Settings

The film takes place on Earth, but spans alternate realities, each with its own rules which even reaches an absurd degree.

Personal Thoughts

The film actively portrays the different cultural realities especially in an Asian perspective. It also showed different contemporary issues within the current population such as Jobu Topaki being a nihilist which may be a portrayal of the current younger generation. The relation of the Evelyn and her daughter also displays the stereotypes of an Asian family household, which isn't necessary true for all but a reality for a considerable amount of the Asian population. Parental issues and homophobia being tackled within the film deeply resonates with me as this is a very common problem I observe within the majority of my acquaintances, typically in a minority group. The film overall left a very deep impression in me and I would recommend it to anybody looking for an action film that also would leave an emotional impact.
